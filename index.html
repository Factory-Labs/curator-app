Design Philosophy

We've explored two different AI assistance approaches to help you evaluate which better preserves editor agency:

Approach A: AI Suggests Scores (With Suggestions)

AI provides specific score suggestions (e.g., "Suggested Score: 3") with reasoning. Editors can accept or modify. Risk: Even with best intentions, editors may anchor to suggested numbers rather than form independent judgments.

Approach B: AI Provides Evidence Only (Recommended)

AI presents factual analysis and evidence without suggesting scores. Comparisons to past evaluations appear only AFTER editor makes their decision. Benefit: Promotes genuine independent thinking while still providing valuable context.

No Suggested Scores

AI presents analysis and evidence, but never suggests specific scores to prevent anchoring bias.

Independent Thinking

Comparisons to past evaluations appear only AFTER editor makes their decision.

Evidence-Based

AI performs automated checks and presents factual findings for editor interpretation.

Transparent Calculations

All scoring formulas and payout calculations are visible and update in real-time.

Key Features Across All Mockups

Data Access: Quick access to source data for verification (View Dataset/Submission Data buttons)

AI Context Panel: Can be hidden/shown at any time, giving editors full control over when they want AI input

Historical Context: Past similar evaluations shown for consistency calibration, but only after initial judgment

Real-time Feedback: Live calculation updates help editors understand the impact of their decisions
